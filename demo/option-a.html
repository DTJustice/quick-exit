Apply for protection

If you need urgent help, call the police on 000.

If you need housing in a women’s refuge, call 1800 811 811.

Making a private application

If you are experiencing domestic violence, you can apply for a domestic violence order using the application for a protection order form.

See what is domestic violence? if you are unsure of what behaviour or relationships are considered by the court to be domestic violence.

To apply, you can either:

  • fill out the interactive PDF form on your computer
  • print the PDF and fill it out by hand
  • go to a Magistrates Court and complete the form there.

If there is not enough space on the application form, you can write or type more information on a separate sheet of paper and attach it to your application form.

Once you’ve completed the form you will need to:

  1. sign the statutory declaration in front of a Justice of the Peace
  2. file the application at a Magistrates Court in Queensland.

There are Justices of the Peace (JPs) at most Magistrates Courts, so you should be able to sign the statutory declaration and file the application in one visit to a court.

Find a courthouse location or, to find out when a JP will be available at your local courthouse (or elsewhere), search for your nearest JP or Cdec.

There is no cost to make an application.

When you file the application you will be given a court date when you are next to go to court.

Difficulty completing the form

The questions on the form include some legal terms that may be hard to understand. If you are having trouble with the form, many courthouses have domestic violence support workers who can support you through the process. The domestic violence support workers are only available at the courthouses at limited times. You may contact a courthouse to see what assistance is available.

Information you need to include in the application

It is important that you answer all the questions on the form and take your time to include as much information as possible. You will need to include details of what domestic violence has happened or been threatened and when and where it occurred.

When describing what the respondent has said to you, try to use their words exactly as you remember them.

Naming children on your application

On your application you will need to list any children who are:

  • your children
  • your unborn children
  • children who spend time at your home on a regular on-going basis.

The form asks you to select if you want each of the children you list named on the domestic violence order—so that if the order is made, the same conditions that apply to you will also apply to them.

If the magistrate decides that the respondent has committed domestic violence against you, they will consider naming any children on the order even if you haven’t selected on the form that you want them named.

Cross applications

If you think the person you are applying for a domestic violence order against has made an application against you (or is planning to), it is important that you tell the court when you lodge your form as the court will try and hear both matters together.

Register an order from another state, territory or country

If you have a domestic violence order in another Australian state or territory or in New Zealand, then you do not need to make another application for protection here. However, you do need to register your order in Queensland.

To register your order complete an Application for registration in Queensland of an interstate domestic violence order form and file it at any Magistrate Court.

Ways to get help

Counselling

For counselling, information and referrals call:

  • DV Connect Womensline on 1800 811 811 (24 hours, 7 days)
  • DV Connect Men’s Line on 1800 600 636 (9am – midnight, 7 days)
  • Kids Help Line 1800 55 1800 (24 hours, 7 days) (if you are between 5 and 25 years old).

For crisis counselling call Lifeline on 13 11 14 (24 hours, 7 days).

Legal advice and lawyers

For free legal advice:

  • call Legal Aid Queensland 1300 65 11 88 (Monday - Friday, 8.30am - 5pm)
  • call Women's Legal Service on 1800 677 278 (Monday, Tuesday and Thursday 9.30am - 1.30pm, Wednesday 1pm - 4pm).

To get a lawyer see Queensland Law Society's find a solicitor tool.

Interpreters

Call the National Translator and Interpreter Service on 13 14 50.

Hearing impaired help

Call National Relay Service on 13 36 77 (TTY users) or 1300 555 727 (speak and listen users).
